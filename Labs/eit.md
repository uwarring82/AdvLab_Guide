## The Pull

Light normally gets absorbed when it passes through a dense atomic medium. But with the right preparation ‚Äî two laser fields, precisely tuned, coherently driving a three-level system ‚Äî you can open a window of transparency exactly where absorption should be strongest. This is Electromagnetically Induced Transparency: a quantum interference effect that makes opaque media transparent, slows light to walking pace, and enables the storage of photons in atomic spin waves.

These are not textbook curiosities. EIT underpins quantum memory protocols, precision magnetometry, and coherent control techniques used across atomic physics. You will build and operate a complete EIT system ‚Äî from laser stabilisation to light storage.

This is not a fixed recipe. This is a developing platform.

---

## Guiding Question

> **How do we establish and optimise the coherent conditions required for EIT in a warm rubidium vapour ‚Äî and what limits the transparency, the magnetic sensitivity, and the light storage fidelity we can achieve?**

## What Makes This Lab Different

You inherit an experimental apparatus that has been built, aligned, and refined by previous student groups. Your task is twofold:

1. **Do science**: Lock a laser to an atomic transition, generate control and signal beams with precise frequency differences, observe EIT resonances, measure magnetic fields via Zeeman shifts, and store light pulses in atomic coherences.

2. **Improve the platform**: Each group encounters different challenges and finds different solutions. We actively welcome your input on optical alignment procedures, electronic filtering, data acquisition workflows, polarisation control, or any aspect of the apparatus that could work better.

This reflects how experimental atomic physics actually operates: you build on accumulated knowledge, you diagnose problems systematically, and you document what you learn for those who follow.

## Safety Reminder

The diode laser is a **Class-3b system** (up to 100 mW at 780 nm). Appropriate laser safety goggles must be worn whenever the laser is operating.

The EIT chamber is temperature-stabilised to approximately **95 ¬∞C**. Do not touch the chamber surfaces.

Do not disconnect acousto-optical modulators while RF amplifiers are powered. Ensure RF input power remains below 10 dBm.
