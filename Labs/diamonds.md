## The Pull

Quantum sensors are reshaping precision measurement. Unlike trapped atoms that demand ultra-high vacuum and cryogenic temperatures, nitrogen-vacancy centres in diamond operate at room temperature, on your benchtop, potentially even inside living cells. You are about to work with one of the most promising platforms for nanoscale magnetometry ‚Äî and the techniques you will learn connect directly to atomic clocks, quantum information, and cutting-edge metrology.

This is not a cookbook experiment. This is a sandbox.

---

## Guiding Question

> **How can we systematically characterise and optimise NV-centre fluorescence and magnetic resonance signals to develop a reliable, sensitive magnetometer ‚Äî and what improvements to the apparatus would make this easier for the next team?**

---

## What Makes This Lab Different

You inherit an evolving experimental setup. Previous student groups have built, calibrated, and refined this apparatus. Your task is twofold:

1. **Do science**: Characterise diamonds, record fluorescence spectra, measure optically detected magnetic resonances (ODMR), and extract quantitative results with proper uncertainty analysis.

2. **Improve the platform**: Each group contributes something different. We actively welcome your input on how to improve the mechanical stability, optical alignment, electronic signal chain, data acquisition workflow, or analysis procedures.

This mirrors how real experimental physics works: you rarely start from scratch, you build on what came before, and you leave things better than you found them.

## Safety Reminder

The diode laser is a **Class-3b system** (up to 100 mW at 519 nm). Appropriate laser safety goggles must be worn whenever the laser is operating. No exceptions.
